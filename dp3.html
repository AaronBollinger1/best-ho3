What a DP-3 Covers

The landlord's coverages, one policy

A dwelling-fire policy protects the building and your rental income — the tenant insures their own belongings. Get these right and the rest is fine print. Full DP-3 guide →

COVERAGE A

Dwelling

The rental structure at rebuild cost — open-perils on a DP-3 special form, the number every other limit hangs off.

COVERAGE B

Other Structures

Detached garages, fences, and sheds on the rental lot — typically a percentage of Coverage A, adjustable to the property.

COVERAGE D / E

Fair Rental Value

Loss of rents — the income you keep collecting while a covered loss makes the unit unrentable. The coverage landlords most often under-buy.

COVERAGE L

Landlord Liability

Defends and pays when a tenant or guest is injured on the premises — a slip on the stairs, a dog on the property. $300k is the floor we discuss.

OPTIONAL

Vandalism & Theft

Vandalism and malicious mischief — often excluded when a unit sits vacant, so it is endorsed deliberately, not assumed.

OPTIONAL

Ordinance or Law

Pays the extra cost to rebuild an older rental to current code — critical on the pre-1980 buildings that fill California's rental stock.

What Drives Your Premium

Six levers set a California landlord premium

Rental risk prices differently than owner-occupied. The levers below are what carriers actually weigh — and where an independent broker earns the spread.

1 · Occupancy & tenancy

Long-term lease, month-to-month, or short-term — each shifts appetite and pricing more than the ZIP does.

2 · Wildfire exposure

The dominant California factor — brush distance and fire-zone mapping decide which markets will write the rental at all.

3 · Coverage A

Premium scales with rebuild cost. Insure the structure to rebuild, not to the price you paid or the rent roll.

4 · Loss of rents

Fair rental value limit and the number of units directly move the premium — and the payout when a unit goes dark.

5 · Age & systems

Roof age, wiring, and plumbing on older rentals push carriers to surcharge, exclude, or decline — documentation reopens doors.

6 · Carrier choice

The same rental prices well apart across admitted and specialty markets. This one is our job.
