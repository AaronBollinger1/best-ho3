Rebuild cost is its own number

Four numbers orbit your house and only one belongs on the policy. Market value includes the land — which survives every fire. Purchase price is history. Assessed value is a tax figure. Rebuild cost — labor, materials, debris removal, and design fees to reconstruct the same home on the same lot — is what Coverage A insures. In much of coastal California rebuild cost sits well below market value; setting the limit from a Zillow estimate is how homes end up under- or over-insured.

Why this is a starting point your broker verifies

A per-square-foot figure is the honest first pass, not the last word. The real rebuild cost moves with construction quality (tract vs custom), number of stories, roof type, hillside access, and finish level — the adjustments a full replacement-cost valuation makes. That's why the number above is explicitly a broker-verified starting point: it gets you a credible target in seconds, and a licensed broker confirms it before your coverage is bound. Is this a quote?

No. It's a preliminary rebuild-cost estimate to anchor your Coverage A. A real quote comes from carriers after underwriting, a replacement-cost valuation, wildfire-zone review, and usually an inspection. Nothing here is a quote, binder, or offer of coverage. More common questions →

Can you actually bind coverage from this?

Coverage is bound by a licensed broker with a carrier — not by a calculator. This tool hands your square footage into the official ACORD 80 application; once you complete and e-sign it, a broker verifies the rebuild cost and takes it to market to place bindable coverage.
